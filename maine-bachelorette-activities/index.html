15 Ideas

The Best Maine Bachelorette Party Activities

From the unmissable to the truly one-of-a-kind. Every activity on this list links back to the real Maine — not a tourist package version of it.

01

Most Popular — Don't Miss

Private Boat Cruise on Casco Bay

The single best bachelorette activity in all of Maine. Board a classic wooden vessel with your entire crew and spend the afternoon or evening sailing the 200+ islands of Casco Bay. Fully private — no strangers, no schedule you didn't set. Choose from four experiences: mimosa brunch, sunset charter, lighthouse sightseeing, or fall foliage cruise. Add catering from Chaval and The Ugly Duckling to any of them.

Groups: Up to 16 guests  |  Duration: 2–4 hours

See the Boat Cruise
02

Only in Portland

World-Class Dining on the Water — Chaval & The Ugly Duckling

Ilma Lopez and Damian Sansonetti are Portland food royalty. Between them they hold multiple James Beard Award nominations, including Best Chef: Northeast and Outstanding Pastry Chef. Their restaurants, Chaval and The Ugly Duckling, are among the most celebrated in New England.

Catering from their kitchens is available on every charter — from Chaval tapas and a buttery brioche lobster roll to handcrafted croissants and pastries. Add it during booking.

Available on: Every private charter — select during booking

03

Active

Sunrise Kayaking on Casco Bay

Guided sunrise kayak tours launch from the Eastern Promenade with views of the harbor, the islands, and the open ocean catching early light. An incredible way to start the day before the main celebration.

04

Most Scenic

Private Island Picnic

Charter the boat out to one of Casco Bay's uninhabited islands, anchor in a cove, and spend the afternoon on a private beach with charcuterie, wine, and views that look like a screensaver. Completely exclusive to your group.

Add-on: Available as part of the private boat charter

05

Food & Drink

Portland Food Tour

Portland has more James Beard Award nominees per capita than almost any city in the country. A guided food tour through the Old Port hits oyster bars, artisan cheese shops, craft beer pours, and lobster rolls in a 2-hour window.

06

Classic Maine

Traditional Maine Lobster Bake

A true Maine lobster bake — whole lobsters, clams, corn, and potatoes cooked over a wood fire on the beach. Catered options are available for private groups and make for an unforgettable bachelorette dinner you'd never have anywhere else.

07

Relaxation

Spa Day in Portland

Portland's boutique spa scene is legitimate. Book a group treatment day at one of the Old Port spas — massages, facials, and champagne — as the recovery morning after the boat cruise.

08

Scenic

Coastal Hike on Cape Elizabeth

The Cliff Walk at Cape Elizabeth is a 30-minute drive from Portland and features dramatic Atlantic coastline views, tidal pools, and Two Lights State Park. The perfect morning activity before an afternoon on the water.

09

Bar Crawl

Old Port Bar Crawl

The Old Port is the perfect bar crawl neighborhood — compact, walkable, and packed with excellent bars from sophisticated cocktail lounges to packed dive bars. Start at Portland Hunt & Alpine and let the night take you from there.

10

Day Trip

Ferry to Peaks Island

A 15-minute ferry ride from the Old Port puts you on Peaks Island — a small community of 800 people with beaches, bike rentals, and a view of Portland that's genuinely stunning. Great Sunday morning activity before heading home.
