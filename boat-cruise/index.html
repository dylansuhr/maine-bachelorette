Board the classic wooden vessel Marie L right from Portland's Old Port and spend the afternoon or evening exploring the 200+ islands of Casco Bay. No strangers. No shared tours. The entire boat is yours.

Common Questions

Bachelorette Boat Cruise FAQ

How many people can join the bachelorette boat cruise?

The charter accommodates up to 16 guests and is 100% private — the entire boat is exclusively yours. No strangers, no shared decks. Perfect for a full bachelorette party crew.

Is the open bar really all-inclusive?

Yes — every charter includes an unlimited open bar at no extra charge. Local Portland beer, hard seltzer, imported white and red wine, rosé, and bubbly — all included in the per-person price. No drink minimums, no bar tabs, no surprise charges.

What is included in the charter?

Every charter includes the vessel Marie L, a licensed captain, and an unlimited open bar — local Portland beer, hard seltzer, imported white and red wine, rosé, and bubbly. All drinks are included in the per-person price from $145. Catering from Chaval and The Ugly Duckling can be added to any charter.

When should I book?

Summer and fall weekends fill up quickly — we recommend booking as far in advance as possible to secure your preferred date. Spring and weekday dates tend to have more availability.

Where does the boat depart from?

The charter departs from Downtown Portland's Old Port — walkable from downtown hotels, restaurants, and bars.

Can we add catering to our charter?

Yes — catering from Portland's acclaimed Chaval and The Ugly Duckling is available on every charter. Select your menu during the booking process or up to 72 hours before departure. You're also welcome to bring your own food and non-alcoholic beverages.

Who is catering the food on the charters?

The catering comes from Ilma Lopez and Damian Sansonetti — the James Beard Award-nominated chefs behind Chaval and The Ugly Duckling, two of Portland's most celebrated restaurants. Their menus feature Chaval-inspired tapas, a knuckle and claw lobster roll, artisan pastries, charcuterie, and premium caviar service.

What should we wear on the boat cruise?

Dress for the weather on the water — it's typically 5–10°F cooler out on Casco Bay than on shore. A light layer or jacket is recommended even in summer. Flat shoes or sneakers are safest on deck; heels aren't recommended on a moving boat.

Is there a bathroom on the boat?

Yes — the Marie L has a composting head on board. Think airplane lavatory — compact but functional for a 2–3 hour cruise.
